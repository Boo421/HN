Ha Noi Pho

49 Bat Dan Street, Hoan Kiem District, Hanoi

Hanoi Pho is a famous delicious dish in Hanoi when it comes to this place's cuisine. A bowl of pho is full, hot, rich in flavor, and beautiful every time it is brought to the table, it always attracts diners. The pho noodles are soft, the broth is sweet and rich in pork bone flavor, the fragrant cilantro, and the meat that comes with the bowl will definitely satisfy you.

Bun Cha Ha Noi

34 Hang Than Street, Ba Dinh District, Hanoi

Bun cha is a Vietnamese dish, including vermicelli, pork patties grilled over charcoal and a bowl of sweet and sour, spicy and salty fish sauce. The dish originates from Northern Vietnam and is the most enduring gift of Hanoi, so it can be considered one of the typical specialties of Hanoi cuisine. Bun cha is similar to grilled meat vermicelli in the Central and South, but the fish sauce mix has a milder taste.

Vermicelli With Tofu And Shrimp Paste

49 Phat Loc Alley, Hoan Kiem District, Hanoi

Vermicelli with tofu and shrimp paste is a simple, rustic dish in Northern Vietnamese cuisine and originates from Hanoi. This is a dish often eaten as a snack.

Cha Ca La Vong

19-21-31 Duong Thanh Street, Hoan Kiem, Hanoi

Cha Ca La Vong is the name of Hanoi's specialty fish cake. This is a dish made from fish sliced ​​into pieces, marinated, grilled on charcoal and then fried again in a greased pan.

Com

19 Lane 85 Xuan Thuy Street, Cau Giay, Hanoi

Com Hanoi is not only a specialty food of the Capital but also a dish associated with autumn, with beautiful, simple, and dear memories of every Hanoi citizen.

Trang Tien Ice Cream

35 Trang Tien Street, Hoan Kiem District, Hanoi

Trang Tien Ice Cream - an ice cream brand that is too close to the people of Hanoi and tourists from near and far. With more than 60 years of production and customer service experience, Trang Tien ice cream has become a symbol of Hanoi's culinary culture.
